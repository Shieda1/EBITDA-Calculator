What is the EBITDA?
EBITDA is a measurement of a firm's profitability from the operating business only before the effects of indebtedness and costs required to mantain the asset base.
How to calculate EBITDA?
To calculate EBITDA you should use the following formula:
EBITDA = net income + taxes + interest expense + depreciation and amortization
example: company x's net income is $35,000,000, the depreciation and amortization is $19,000,000, interest expenses of $8,000,000 and $7,300,000 in taxes, calculate the EBITDA of this company.
Using the EBITDA formula the answer will be:
EBITDA = $35,000,000 + $7,300,000 + $8,000,000 + $19,000,000
EBITDA = $69,300,000
Why use EBITDA?
EBITDA is usually used to give a good idea of a firm's value. It is also used by investors to understand the firm's worth.
